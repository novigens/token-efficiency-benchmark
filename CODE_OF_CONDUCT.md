# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, or experience level. We are building shared evaluation infrastructure; the value of that work depends on the project being a place where people across organizations and viewpoints can contribute on the technical merits of their work.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment:

- Focusing discussion on the design and the code, not on people.
- Giving credit for prior work, even where it disagrees with our approach.
- Receiving constructive criticism gracefully and giving it precisely.
- Assuming good faith from other contributors when their reasoning is not yet clear.
- Acknowledging when one is wrong and updating publicly.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, design reviews, or releases.
- Misrepresenting benchmark methodology or results for promotional purposes.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

Instances of abusive or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the maintainers at the address in `MAINTAINERS.md`. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.
